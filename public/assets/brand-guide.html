CORELOT Homes Brand Guide

Our brand standards, visual identity, and communication guidelines for creating consistent, authentic CORELOT experiences.

Core Brand Traits

These five traits define who we are and how we show up in every interaction, from initial contact through closing and beyond. We embody these traits in our communications, customer service, and all marketing materials.

Straightforward (Politely)

We communicate clearly and directly without industry jargon or hidden costs. We believe our clients deserve honest, plain-language explanations about timelines, pricing, and processes. We're transparent about what we can and cannot do.

Integrity (Small Things)

We honor commitments, return calls promptly, and follow through on details. The small gestures—showing up on time, keeping job sites clean, answering questions thoroughly—demonstrate our commitment to doing what's right.

Detail-Oriented (Not Overwhelming)

We sweat the small stuff so our clients don't have to. From precise site plans to carefully reviewed contracts, we're thorough. But we present information clearly, focusing on what matters most to homeowners.

Friendly (Not Pushy)

We're here to help families build where they belong, not to pressure them into decisions. We answer questions, provide guidance, and give clients the space to make confident choices on their timeline.

Sincere (Always)

We mean what we say. Our enthusiasm for helping people build homes is genuine. We celebrate our clients' milestones and treat every project with the care it deserves—because we understand what homeownership means.

Typography Standards

Our typography system uses Calibri throughout all brand materials. This creates consistency and ensures our communications are accessible and easy to read across digital and print formats.

Font Usage Guidelines

  • Titles and Headlines: Calibri Bold (700 weight) for maximum impact and hierarchy
  • Highlights and Emphasis: Calibri Italic for subtle emphasis within body text
  • Body Text: Calibri Light (300 weight) or regular Calibri for comfortable readability
  • Digital Fallback: Calibri, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif

Note: Calibri is widely available on Windows and Mac systems. For web applications, we use a carefully selected sans-serif fallback stack to ensure consistent appearance across all devices.

Color Palette

Our color palette is intentionally simple and bold. These three core colors define the CORELOT visual identity and should be used consistently across all materials.

Primary Orange

#cc5500

Our signature brand color. Use for headlines, CTAs, accents, and brand elements. This warm, energetic orange conveys approachability and action.

Usage: Buttons, headlines, links, logo, key visual elements

Primary Black

#000000

Our primary text color for body copy and detailed information. Pure black provides maximum contrast and readability across all materials.

Usage: Body text, descriptions, technical information, captions

Supporting Gray

#888888

Our neutral supporting color for backgrounds, contrast elements, and secondary information. Provides visual breathing room without competing with primary content.

Usage: Backgrounds, borders, secondary text, subtle accents

Color Accessibility

All color combinations in our brand palette meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards for contrast and readability. When using orange on white or black on white, ensure sufficient contrast for all users.

Logo Usage Guidelines

Our logo is the most recognizable element of our brand identity. Consistent, respectful use of the CORELOT Homes logo ensures brand recognition and professional presentation.

Approved Logo Formats

  • Horizontal Logo: Primary logo for most applications—websites, letterhead, email signatures, presentations
  • CORELOT Symbol: Square icon format for social media profiles, favicons, app icons, and space-constrained applications
  • Tagline Version: Available for marketing materials where additional context is valuable

Logo Usage Rules

  • DO: Maintain clear space around the logo (minimum 1/4 logo height on all sides)
  • DO: Use approved logo files in vector format (SVG, EPS) when possible
  • DO: Ensure logos appear on appropriate backgrounds (white, light gray, or photographic)
  • DON'T: Alter logo colors, stretch, distort, or rotate the logo
  • DON'T: Add effects like shadows, outlines, or gradients to the logo
  • DON'T: Place logo on busy backgrounds where it loses clarity

Equal Housing Opportunity (EHO) Icon

All public-facing marketing materials, websites, printed brochures, advertisements, and signage must include the Equal Housing Opportunity icon. This demonstrates our commitment to fair housing practices and legal compliance.

Placement: Typically positioned in footers, bottom corners, or alongside contact information. The EHO icon should be clearly visible but not compete with primary content.

Brand Voice & Messaging

Our brand voice reflects our core traits in every piece of content we create. We write in first person plural (we/our) to emphasize our partnership with clients throughout their building journey.

Voice Characteristics

  • Conversational but professional
  • Confident without being boastful
  • Educational and helpful
  • Warm and approachable
  • Action-oriented and clear

Tone Variations

  • Educational Content: Informative, patient, thorough
  • Marketing Materials: Enthusiastic, inviting, inspiring
  • Technical Documents: Precise, clear, professional
  • Customer Service: Helpful, understanding, responsive

Word Choice Guidelines

We prefer: home (not house), family, community, customize, partner with, guide, transparent, straightforward

We avoid: luxury, exclusive, elite, complicated jargon, vague promises, high-pressure language

Applying Brand Standards

These guidelines come to life in everything we create—from business cards to billboards, email templates to house plans. Consistency builds trust and recognition.
