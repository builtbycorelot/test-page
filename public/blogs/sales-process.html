Our Sales and Construction Process

A clear roadmap from first contact through move-in day and beyond

Understanding the Journey

Building a custom home is a significant undertaking, and knowing what to expect makes the process less stressful and more enjoyable. We've refined our approach over years of projects to create a clear, systematic process that keeps you informed and engaged every step of the way.

This isn't a mystery. There are no hidden steps or surprise requirements. From the moment you first contact us through your final walkthrough and beyond, you'll know exactly where we are in the process and what comes next.

Let's walk through the entire journey together.

Phase 1: Initial Contact and Discovery

What Happens

You reach out—maybe through our website, a phone call, or a referral from someone who's worked with us. We schedule an initial consultation at a time that works for you. This meeting can happen at our Jefferson Barn design studio, at your property if you already own land, or virtually if that's more convenient.

What We Discuss

  • Your vision: What kind of home are you imagining? What features matter most?
  • Your timeline: When do you need to move in? Are there any deadline constraints?
  • Your budget: What's your realistic financial capacity? Have you been pre-qualified for financing?
  • Your property: Do you own land? Are you looking for a lot? What locations interest you?
  • Your priorities: What's non-negotiable versus nice-to-have?

What You Can Expect From Us

We listen more than we talk. This isn't a sales pitch—it's a conversation to understand whether we're the right fit for your project and whether we can deliver what you need within your budget and timeline. We'll be honest if we see potential challenges or misalignments. It's better to identify these early than discover them months into the process.

Timeline: Initial consultation typically lasts 60-90 minutes. No commitment required.

Phase 2: Lot Evaluation and Feasibility

If You Own Land

We evaluate your property to understand its characteristics and construction implications. We look at topography, soil conditions, utility access, setback requirements, easements, and any constraints that might affect design or cost. We may order a survey or perc test if current information is inadequate.

This evaluation helps us understand site-specific costs before we get too deep into design. A beautiful lot with challenging conditions might require additional foundation work, longer utility runs, or extensive grading—all of which affect budget.

If You're Looking for Land

We help you search for suitable lots in your target area and price range. We can evaluate properties you're considering, accompany you to viewings, and provide guidance on what makes a lot good or problematic from a building perspective. We have relationships with land sellers and sometimes know about lots before they hit the public market.

The Feasibility Assessment

Once we've evaluated a specific lot, we provide preliminary feedback on feasibility: Can we build what you want within your budget on this property? Are there any deal-breakers? What opportunities does the lot offer?

If the lot works, we move forward. If it doesn't, we explain why and help you find better options. We'd rather have this conversation now than after you've purchased land that doesn't suit your goals.

Timeline: Lot evaluation typically takes 1-2 weeks depending on whether additional testing or surveys are needed.

Phase 3: Design Development and Planning

Plan Selection or Customization

We review our WL Martin plans to see if any align with your vision. These proven designs can be used as-is or modified to suit your needs. If you prefer a fully custom design, we start with your wish list and develop a plan from scratch.

This phase involves sketches, floor plan iterations, and discussions about layout, flow, room sizes, and special features. We use both traditional methods and 3D visualization to help you understand how the space will actually feel.

Material and Finish Selections

We walk through finish options—flooring, countertops, cabinets, fixtures, hardware, lighting, and more. We provide guidance on what's included in base pricing versus upgrade options. You can see and touch samples at our design studio.

We help you prioritize where to invest and where to save. Maybe you care deeply about kitchen finishes but are fine with standard bathroom fixtures. We tailor selections to your priorities and budget.

Engineering and Refinement

We work with engineers to ensure structural integrity, proper systems sizing, and code compliance. We refine the design based on engineering feedback and your evolving vision. This iterative process continues until you're satisfied with the plan.

Timeline: Design development typically takes 3-6 weeks depending on complexity and how quickly decisions are made. We move at your pace—no rushing, no pressure.

Phase 4: Detailed Proposal and Contract

The Proposal

We provide a comprehensive, itemized proposal that includes:

  • Base construction costs broken down by category
  • Site-specific costs (clearing, grading, utilities, etc.)
  • Finish selections and any upgrade costs
  • Allowances for items to be selected later
  • Timeline estimates from groundbreaking to completion
  • Payment schedule aligned with construction milestones
  • Warranty coverage details

Review and Questions

You review the proposal carefully. We encourage questions—lots of them. We want you to understand every line item, every assumption, every cost. If something doesn't make sense or align with your expectations, we discuss it openly.

We may revise the proposal based on your feedback. Perhaps you want to adjust finish levels, modify the design, or explore value engineering options. This is the time to refine until everything aligns with your vision and budget.

Contract Signing

Once you're comfortable with the proposal, we prepare a detailed construction contract. The contract formalizes the scope, pricing, timeline, payment schedule, and responsibilities. We review it together to ensure mutual understanding before signing.

Timeline: From proposal delivery to contract signing typically takes 1-2 weeks, though some clients need more time to review or arrange financing. There's no rush.

Phase 5: Permitting and Pre-Construction

Permitting Process

We handle all permit applications with the local building department. This includes submitting engineered plans, responding to any reviewer comments, and obtaining all necessary approvals. Permitting timelines vary by jurisdiction but typically take 4-8 weeks.

Pre-Construction Activities

While permits are pending, we finalize any remaining material selections, order long-lead-time items, schedule subcontractors, and prepare the job site. We coordinate utility connections, arrange for temporary power, and confirm all logistics are in place.

Pre-Construction Meeting

Before breaking ground, we meet to review the construction schedule, discuss communication protocols, and address any final questions. We want you to feel confident and informed as we begin construction.

Timeline: Permitting and pre-construction typically take 6-10 weeks depending on the jurisdiction and project complexity.

Phase 6: Construction

Site Preparation and Foundation

We clear and grade the site, install erosion controls, dig the foundation, install footings and foundation walls, and complete waterproofing and drainage systems. This phase typically takes 2-4 weeks depending on weather and soil conditions.

Framing and Roof

We frame the structure, install sheathing, complete the roof system, and install windows and exterior doors. The house starts to take shape and you can begin to visualize the space. This phase typically takes 3-5 weeks.

Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing (MEP)

We rough-in all systems—HVAC ductwork, electrical wiring, plumbing lines. Inspections occur at various stages to ensure code compliance. This phase typically takes 2-3 weeks.

Insulation and Drywall

We install insulation, hang and finish drywall, and apply texture where specified. The interior starts to feel like actual rooms rather than framed spaces. This phase typically takes 3-4 weeks.

Interior Finishes

We install trim, cabinets, countertops, flooring, tile, fixtures, hardware, and paint. This is where your finish selections come to life. This phase typically takes 4-6 weeks.

Exterior Finishes and Site Work

We complete siding or exterior finish, install gutters and downspouts, complete decks or porches, grade and seed the yard, and install the driveway. This work often overlaps with interior finishes. Typically 3-4 weeks.

Communication During Construction

We provide regular updates throughout construction. You're welcome to visit the site anytime (we recommend coordinating visits for safety). We reach out proactively when decisions are needed or when we encounter anything unexpected. You'll never wonder what's happening or feel disconnected from the process.

Total Construction Timeline: Typical projects take 6-9 months from groundbreaking to completion, depending on size, complexity, and weather.

Phase 7: Final Walkthrough and Closing

Pre-Final Walkthrough

Before the official final walkthrough, we conduct our own punch-list inspection to catch and address any issues. We want the home to be as close to perfect as possible when you see it.

Your Final Walkthrough

We walk through the completed home together, room by room. We explain how systems work, point out important features, and create a punch-list of any items that need attention. Most items are minor—a paint touch-up, adjusting a cabinet door, etc.

Punch-List Completion

We address all punch-list items promptly. Once everything is complete, we schedule a second walkthrough to confirm resolution.

Closing and Move-In

We coordinate with your lender to schedule closing. You receive all documentation, warranties, and keys. The home is officially yours. We celebrate this milestone with you—it's the culmination of months of work and partnership.

Timeline: From initial walkthrough to closing typically takes 1-2 weeks.

Phase 8: Post-Closing and Warranty Support

We Don't Disappear

Our relationship doesn't end at closing. We provide comprehensive warranty coverage and remain available to address any concerns. We want you to feel supported as you settle into your new home.

Warranty Coverage

Our warranty includes one year of coverage for workmanship and materials, two years for mechanical systems, and ten years for structural elements. We explain what's covered, how to submit warranty requests, and what response times to expect.

Seasonal Check-Ins

We often follow up seasonally during the first year to see how everything is performing and address any questions. You're living in the home through all seasons and using all systems—we want to make sure everything meets expectations.

Long-Term Relationship

Many of our clients return to us for additions, renovations, or second homes. Some refer friends and family. We value these ongoing relationships and remain available even beyond warranty periods for guidance or questions.

Timeline: Warranty support continues for years, and our relationship often extends far beyond.

What Makes Our Process Different

Other builders might follow similar general phases, but here's what sets our process apart:

  • Transparency: No hidden steps, costs, or expectations. You know what's happening and why.
  • Communication: We update you proactively, not just when you ask. You're never left wondering.
  • Education: We explain things along the way so you understand the decisions and trade-offs.
  • Flexibility: We move at your pace. No rushing decisions or pressuring timelines.
  • Partnership: This is a collaboration, not a transaction. We're in this together.
  • Quality focus: Every phase emphasizes doing things right, not just fast.
  • Long-term thinking: We build for your satisfaction years from now, not just closing day.
